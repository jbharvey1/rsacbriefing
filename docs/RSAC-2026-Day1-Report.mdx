## Big Story: Federal Agencies Boycott RSAC 2026

**CISA, FBI, and NSA all declined to participate** — a significant and unprecedented absence at the industry's largest gathering.

**Why it happened:** RSAC hired **Jen Easterly** (former CISA Director under Biden, 2021–2025) as its new CEO. Easterly had publicly criticized Trump's loyalty mandate at RSAC 2025. Federal agencies withdrew from the conference roster within days of her appointment.

**Official CISA statement:** *"We will not participate in the RSA Conference since we regularly review all stakeholder engagements, to ensure maximum impact and good stewardship of taxpayer dollars."*

**Industry reaction:** Strong backlash. Security executives and analysts described the absence as a dangerous retreat from public-private threat coordination — especially given rising nation-state attacks and AI-driven threats. For over a decade, federal presence at RSAC has signaled the government's commitment to collaborative defense.

## IBM at RSAC 2026

IBM is one of the major anchors at the show this year, with a heavy focus on **autonomous security operations and agentic AI**.

### ATOM — Autonomous Threat Operations Machine
IBM's flagship announcement: **ATOM** is an agentic AI system designed for fully autonomous threat triage, investigation, and remediation with minimal human intervention.

- Acts as a **vendor-agnostic digital operator** within IBM's TDR platform
- Integrates with Google Cloud, Microsoft, and IBM's own tooling
- Orchestrates multiple individual agents to accelerate detection, enrich alerts, execute investigation plans, and perform remediation

### X-Force Predictive Threat Intelligence (PTI) Agent
- New agent added to ATOM
- Built on **industry vertical-specific AI foundation models**
- Ingests 100+ data sources (X-Force TI, open-source RSS, APIs, org context)
- Generates predictive threat insights on adversary behavior before incidents occur
- Reduces manual threat hunting

### IBM 2026 X-Force Threat Intelligence Index (Released Feb 25, 2026)
Key findings being showcased at RSAC:
- **44% increase** in attacks exploiting public-facing applications
- **Vulnerability exploitation** is now the #1 cause of incidents (40% of X-Force cases)
- **49% surge** in active ransomware/extortion groups year-over-year
- AI is enabling faster vulnerability discovery and weaponization

### Other IBM Themes at the Show
- **IBM Verify + HashiCorp Vault**: Unified identity governance for human and machine identities across hybrid/multi-cloud
- **Quantum-Safe Readiness**: Sessions on transitioning to quantum-resistant encryption
- Focus on **data and AI pipeline security**, **hybrid cloud workload protection**

## Key Themes Across the Show Floor

1. **Agentic AI is the dominant story** — every major vendor is announcing AI agents that reason, decide, and act autonomously in the SOC
2. **Non-human identity management** — AI agents, service accounts, and machine identities are the new attack surface
3. **Autonomous SOC** — shift from human-reactive to AI-proactive defense; analysts move to strategy and oversight
4. **Platform consolidation** — CISOs are pushing back on tool sprawl; vendors responding with unified platforms
5. **Quantum-safe readiness** — IBM and others pushing enterprise transitions to post-quantum cryptography
6. **Government-private sector friction** — CISA/FBI/NSA absence is a defining narrative of RSAC 2026

## Summary / TL;DR

RSAC 2026 opened today with **agentic AI as the undisputed centerpiece** — every major vendor (IBM, Google, Cisco/Splunk, CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, and a wave of startups) is positioning around autonomous SOC capabilities. IBM's **ATOM** and **X-Force PTI** are their headline plays. The **Innovation Sandbox** is dominated by agentic AI governance and non-human identity startups. The shadow over the event is the unprecedented **boycott by CISA, FBI, and NSA**, driven by political friction over Jen Easterly's appointment as RSAC CEO — a story the security community is watching closely for what it signals about the future of public-private threat coordination.
